Shree Ram is one of the most revered figures in <i><b>Hindu tradition</b></i> and is considered the seventh avatar of Vishnu. He is the central hero of the ancient epic Ramayana, traditionally attributed to Valmiki. Born in the kingdom of Ayodhya as the son of King Dasharatha,<blockquote><i><b> Ram is celebrated as the ideal king, son, husband, and warrior.</i></b></blockquote> His life story highlights virtues such as righteousness (dharma), courage, compassion, and devotion to duty. The epic narrates his 14-year exile, the abduction of his wife Sita by the demon king Ravana, and Ram’s determined quest to rescue her with the help of allies like Hanuman and his devoted brother Lakshmana. Beyond mythology, <section>Ram symbolizes moral strength and righteous leadership, and his story continues to inspire millions through festivals like Diwali</section>, which celebrates his return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana.</p>
